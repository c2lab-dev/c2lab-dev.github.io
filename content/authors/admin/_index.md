**Olivia Chen** is an Associate Professor at Kyushu University, where she directs the **C2Lab (Cryogenic Computing Laboratory)**. Her research focuses on end-to-end superconducting digital computing, spanning device technologies, circuit/architecture design, and full-stack EDA workflows for scalable cryogenic systems.

## Current Positions

- **Associate Professor**, Kyushu University (2024 - Present)
  - Graduate School of Information Science and Electrical Engineering
  - Director, C2Lab (Cryogenic Computing Laboratory)
- **Visiting Associate Professor**, University of California, Riverside (2024 - Present)
- **Visiting Associate Professor**, Yokohama National University (2021 - Present)
- **Founder and Representative Director**, [NPO Aozorium](https://aozorium.org) (2025 - Present)

## Previous Positions

- Associate Professor, Tokyo City University (2021 - 2024)
- Project Assistant Professor, Yokohama National University (2017 - 2021)

## Selected Awards

- **Van Duzer Prize Award**, IEEE Council on Superconductivity (2024)
- **Outstanding Research Award**, Tokyo City University (2023)
- **Award for External Research Funding Achievement**, Yokohama National University (2020)
- **Young Professional Award**, Cryogenic Engineering Conference (2019)

## Research Funding

Principal Investigator on multiple competitive grants including JST FOREST Program (2023-2030), JSPS KAKENHI Grant-in-Aid for Scientific Research (B) (2023-2028), and JST PRESTO (2019-2023).
